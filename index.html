Why E-Waste is a Crisis

Electronic waste (e-waste) is one of the fastest-growing waste streams globally, posing severe environmental and health risks. Discarded devices contain toxic materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium, which can leach into soil and water, harming ecosystems and human health. Many outdated electronics are improperly recycled or dumped in landfills, exacerbating pollution. Additionally, rapid tech advancements and short product lifespans fuel excessive consumption, worsening the crisis. Addressing e-waste requires sustainable disposal, responsible recycling, and conscious consumer choices to reduce its harmful impact.
